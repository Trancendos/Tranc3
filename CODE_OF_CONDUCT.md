Infinity-Trancendos Network Code of Conduct

1. Our Philosophy & Purpose

Like the technical community as a whole, the Infinity-Trancendos Network team and community is made up of a mixture of professionals and volunteers from all over the world, working on every aspect of the mission—including mentorship, teaching, and connecting people.

This isn’t an exhaustive list of things that you can’t do. Rather, take it in the spirit in which it’s intended: a guide to make it easier to enrich all of us and the technical communities in which we participate. We believe that a healthy, innovative ecosystem is sustained by psychological safety, cognitive ease, and mutual respect. This code applies equally to founders, mentors, contributors, and those seeking help and guidance.

2. Our Pledge

Diversity is one of our huge strengths. We pledge to make our community welcoming, safe, and equitable for all.

We are committed to fostering an environment that respects and promotes the dignity, rights, and contributions of all individuals. This includes, but is not limited to, members of any race, ethnicity, culture, national or social origin, immigration status, social and economic class, educational level, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, age, size, family status, political belief, religion, neurodiversity, and mental and physical ability.

The same privileges of participation are extended to everyone who participates in good faith and in accordance with this Covenant.

3. Engineered for Inclusion: Encouraged Behaviors

We recognize that our community spans diverse cultures, backgrounds, and neurotypes. We actively encourage:

Consideration for the Network: Your work will be used by other people, and you in turn will depend on the work of others. Any decision you take will affect users and colleagues, and you should take those consequences into account.

Constructive Disagreement: Disagreements happen all the time. When we disagree, try to understand why. Different people have different perspectives. Being unable to understand why someone holds a viewpoint doesn’t mean that they’re wrong. Remember that it is human to err; focus on resolving issues rather than assigning blame.

Assuming Positive Intent: Start from the assumption that community members are participating in good faith. Ask for clarification before reacting to perceived slights.

Embracing Neurodiversity: Recognize that directness is not inherently rude, and a lack of emotional affect is not inherently hostile. We focus on the substance of contributions over the performative delivery of them.

Owning Your Impact: Understanding that impact matters more than intent. If told your behavior is causing harm, listen with empathy, avoid defensiveness, and commit to repairing that harm.

4. System Threats: Restricted Behaviors

Instances, threats, and promotion of the following behaviors threaten the security and well-being of our community and are strict violations:

Harassment & Sea-lioning: Violating expressed boundaries, persistent unwanted personal attention, or coordinated harassment. This includes "sea-lioning" (relentless, bad-faith requests for evidence designed to exhaust someone).

Character Attacks & Insults: Demeaning comments directed at individuals or groups, especially those using racist or sexist terms.

Microaggressions: Subtle, often unintentional behaviors or comments that communicate hostile or negative attitudes toward marginalized groups.

Tone Policing: Deflecting away from a valid technical point or grievance by attacking the emotional tone or communication style of the person raising it.

Weaponized Technicalities: Using pedantry or hyper-focus on minor errors to belittle others or derail consensus.

Endangerment & Doxxing: Threatening violence, sharing private information without consent, or logging online activity for harassment.

Unwelcoming Sexualization: Posting sexually explicit material or behaving in a way that is inappropriately intimate in a professional context.

5. AI and Automation Ethics

In a modern ecosystem, we welcome the use of generative AI and automation as tools for efficiency, provided they are used with integrity.

Total Ownership: You are 100% responsible for any code, text, or content you submit, regardless of whether an AI assisted in generating it.

No "Bot-splaining": Do not use AI to generate massive, authoritative-sounding replies without verifying the accuracy and relevance of the output.

Signal-to-Noise Ratio: Submitting high volumes of unverified, AI-generated PRs, issues, or comments constitutes spam and will result in an immediate suspension.

6. Scope

This code of conduct applies to all spaces managed by the Infinity-Trancendos Network project or The Foundation. This includes:

Repositories, issue trackers, forums, and Discord/Slack/IRC channels.

At all official events, both online and offline.

When an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

Off-Platform Contexts: Violations of this code outside these spaces may affect a person's ability to participate within them, if that behavior poses a direct threat to the safety and health of our community members.

7. Reporting an Issue

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, believe someone is violating the code of conduct, or have any other concerns, we ask that you report it immediately:

Email: Policy@trancendos.com

More Details: www.trancendos.com/Policies/CodeOfConduct

All reports will be handled with strict confidentiality. Forcing a reporter to confront a harasser puts them in a compromising position; our moderation team will handle direct communication with the reported individual.

8. Addressing and Repairing Harm (Enforcement)

When a violation occurs, our primary goal is to repair harm and maintain community trust. The moderation team uses the following restorative ladder:

Correction & Clarification: A private conversation to point out the behavior, clarify community norms, and request a change in behavior. Used for minor, usually unintentional offenses.

Warning & Cooldown: A formal written warning, often accompanied by a temporary, localized restriction to allow tensions to cool.

Temporary Suspension: A temporary removal from all community spaces. The return from suspension requires the individual to acknowledge the harm caused and agree to specific conditions.

Permanent Ban: Immediate, permanent removal from the ecosystem. Reserved for severe violations or a persistent refusal to align with community standards.
